## Strategic Overview

### Brand Positioning in Advertising
Brandora occupies the "accessible professional brand strategy" position—delivering agency-quality frameworks at startup-friendly pricing through AI-powered technology.

**Core Value Propositions to Communicate:**
1. **Quality:** Same frameworks consultants use
2. **Speed:** 30 minutes vs. 3-6 months
3. **Price:** $49-$149 vs. $15K-$50K
4. **Implementation:** Ready-to-use templates, not just PDF documents

### Campaign Objectives

**Primary Objective:** Drive qualified signups for free brand assessment

**Secondary Objectives:**
- Build brand awareness in startup/founder community
- Position as thought leader in brand strategy space
- Generate qualified leads for Pro tier conversion
- Create content flywheel (educational → product interest)

**Success Metrics:**
- CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost): Target <$200
- LTV:CAC ratio: Target >3:1
- Assessment completion rate: Target >60%
- Assessment-to-paid conversion: Target >15%
- MQL to SQL conversion: Target >30%

## Channel Strategy

### Google Ads (Search)

**Role:** Bottom-funnel, high-intent capture

**Budget Allocation:** 30% of total ad spend

**Strategy:**
- Target problem-aware and solution-aware searchers
- Bid on brand strategy, positioning, and agency alternative keywords
- Run 24/7 with bid adjustments for high-converting hours
- Focus on conversions, not just clicks

**Campaign Structure:**
1. **Brand Strategy Keywords** (40% of Google budget)
   - Broad positioning terms
   - High volume, moderate competition
   - Target: Assessment signups

2. **DIY Branding Keywords** (20% of Google budget)
   - How-to and learning intent
   - Educational angle
   - Target: Content + assessment

3. **Agency Alternative Keywords** (25% of Google budget)
   - Cost-conscious searchers
   - Direct comparison intent
   - Target: Paid conversions

4. **Competitor Keywords** (10% of Google budget)
   - Branded competitor terms
   - Comparison searchers
   - Target: Evaluation stage leads

5. **Problem-Aware Keywords** (5% of Google budget)
   - Struggling with differentiation
   - Need positioning help
   - Target: Problem awareness to solution

**Launch Timeline:**
- Week 1-2: Campaign 1 + 2 only
- Week 3-4: Add Campaign 3
- Week 5+: Add Campaigns 4 + 5 based on performance

### Meta Ads (Facebook/Instagram)

**Role:** Mid-to-top funnel awareness, education, nurture

**Budget Allocation:** 25% of total ad spend

**Strategy:**
- Target cold audiences with educational content
- Retarget warm audiences with product messaging
- Build lookalike audiences from best customers
- Test creative variations constantly

**Campaign Structure:**
1. **Brand Awareness - Cold** (30% of Meta budget)
   - Interest-based targeting (startup, entrepreneurship, marketing)
   - Educational video and carousel ads
   - Objective: Build awareness, drive content engagement

2. **Lead Generation - Warm** (25% of Meta budget)
   - Engagement-based audiences
   - Lead form ads for assessment
   - Objective: Capture leads with instant forms

3. **Retargeting - Hot** (35% of Meta budget)
   - Website visitors, video viewers, assessment starters
   - Direct conversion messaging
   - Objective: Convert warm traffic

4. **Lookalike Expansion** (10% of Meta budget)
   - Lookalike audiences from customers
   - Scale what's working
   - Objective: Efficient acquisition growth

**Creative Testing:**
- New creative every 2 weeks
- 3-5 variations per campaign
- Kill low performers after 7 days

### LinkedIn Ads

**Role:** B2B targeting, thought leadership, enterprise prospects

**Budget Allocation:** 20% of total ad spend

**Strategy:**
- Professional, value-driven messaging
- Target by job title and company size
- Thought leadership content for awareness
- Direct offers for conversion

**Campaign Structure:**
1. **Sponsored Content - Awareness** (40% of LinkedIn budget)
   - Thought leadership posts
   - Industry insights
   - Target: Marketing leaders, founders

2. **Message Ads - Engagement** (15% of LinkedIn budget)
   - Personalized outreach
   - Specific audience segments
   - Target: Decision makers

3. **Document Ads - Education** (20% of LinkedIn budget)
   - Playbooks and guides
   - Lead magnets
   - Target: Information seekers

4. **Video Ads - Consideration** (25% of LinkedIn budget)
   - Platform demos
   - Customer stories
   - Target: Evaluation stage

**Targeting Priority:**
- Job Titles: Founder, CEO, CMO, VP Marketing, Marketing Manager
- Company Size: 1-50, 51-200 (SMB focus)
- Industries: SaaS, Professional Services, E-commerce, Agencies
- Seniority: Director level and above

---

### Twitter/X Ads

**Role:** Thought leadership, startup community engagement

**Budget Allocation:** 10% of total ad spend

**Strategy:**
- Quick-hit thought leadership
- Engage startup Twitter community
- Drive to longer-form content or assessment
- Test thread and video formats

**Campaign Types:**
1. **Promoted Tweets** (60% of Twitter budget)
   - Single tweets, threads, polls
   - Engagement objective
   - Target: Startup community

2. **Video Views** (30% of Twitter budget)
   - Short-form video content
   - Awareness and education
   - Target: Marketing professionals

3. **Website Clicks** (10% of Twitter budget)
   - Direct response
   - Assessment and content
   - Target: High-intent followers

---

### Display Advertising (GDN/Programmatic)

**Role:** Retargeting, brand presence, remarketing

**Budget Allocation:** 10% of total ad spend

**Strategy:**
- Focus almost entirely on retargeting
- Stay top-of-mind with site visitors
- Reinforce messaging across web
- Sequential messaging strategy

**Campaign Structure:**
1. **Homepage Visitors** (30% of display budget)
   - Visited but didn't engage
   - Educational messaging
   - Target: Assessment CTA

2. **Assessment Starters** (35% of display budget)
   - Started but didn't finish
   - Completion messaging
   - Target: Return to assessment

3. **Pricing Viewers** (20% of display budget)
   - Viewed pricing but didn't buy
   - Value and ROI messaging
   - Target: Conversion

4. **Content Engagers** (10% of display budget)
   - Blog/video consumers
   - Apply learning messaging
   - Target: Assessment or lead magnet

5. **Multi-Touch** (5% of display budget)
   - 3+ touchpoints without conversion
   - Different angle/offer
   - Target: Break through resistance

### Organic Social (Supporting Paid)

**Role:** Community building, thought leadership, content distribution

**Budget:** Time investment, not ad spend

**Strategy:**
- Create content that can be amplified with ads
- Build organic presence that paid ads benefit from
- Engage with community (don't just broadcast)
- Test content organically before paid promotion

**Platform Priority:**
1. LinkedIn (primary)
2. Twitter (secondary)
3. Instagram (tertiary)
4. Facebook (minimal)

### Lookalike Audiences

**Build from:**
1. **Customer list** (email addresses of paid customers) - Most valuable
2. **Assessment completers** (engaged, qualified)
3. **High LTV customers** (Pro tier annual subscribers)
4. **Email subscribers** (engaged audience)
5. **Website visitors - high intent pages** (pricing, features)

**Similarity Level:**
- Start with 1% (most similar)
- Expand to 2-5% after proving out
- Avoid 6-10% (too broad, less qualified)

---

### Exclusions

**Always Exclude:**
- Current customers (unless upsell campaign)
- Job seekers (people searching for "brand strategist jobs")
- Students researching for assignments
- Competitors (identified list)
- Impossible-to-convert (identified through analytics)

## Funnel & Customer Journey

### Awareness Stage

**What They Know:** Have a business, maybe know positioning matters

**Content Types:**
- Educational blog posts on brand strategy
- "Why brand positioning matters" videos
- Industry stats and research
- Problem identification content

**Ad Types:**
- Thought leadership on LinkedIn
- Educational videos on Meta
- Content promotion on Twitter
- Long-tail problem keywords on Google

**Goal:** Problem awareness + brand consideration

**Metrics:** Impressions, reach, engagement rate, video views

---

### Consideration Stage

**What They Know:** Need brand strategy, evaluating options

**Content Types:**
- Platform explainers and demos
- Comparison content (vs. agencies, DIY, etc.)
- Customer success stories
- Framework previews

**Ad Types:**
- Product-focused LinkedIn sponsored content
- Retargeting ads on Meta/Display
- Comparison keywords on Google
- Video demos and testimonials

**Goal:** Product consideration + assessment start

**Metrics:** Website visits, video completion rate, content downloads, assessment starts

---

### Decision Stage

**What They Know:** Considering Brandora specifically

**Content Types:**
- Assessment completion prompts
- Pricing and value communication
- Risk reduction (guarantee, testimonials)
- Direct ROI comparisons

**Ad Types:**
- Retargeting (assessment starters, pricing viewers)
- High-intent keywords (branded, competitor)
- Direct response Meta ads
- LinkedIn message ads to decision makers

**Goal:** Assessment completion + purchase

**Metrics:** Assessment completions, trials started, purchases, CAC

---

### Retention/Expansion

**What They Are:** Current customers

**Content Types:**
- Feature education
- Success tips and best practices
- New template announcements
- Upgrade benefits

**Ad Types:**
- Email nurture (not paid ads)
- In-app messaging
- Retargeting for feature awareness
- Upsell campaigns (Starter → Pro)

**Goal:** Activation, retention, expansion

**Metrics:** Usage frequency, feature adoption, upgrade rate, LTV

## Budget Allocation

### Monthly Ad Spend: $20,000 Example

**Channel Distribution:**
- Google Ads: $6,000 (30%)
- Meta Ads: $5,000 (25%)
- LinkedIn Ads: $4,000 (20%)
- Twitter Ads: $2,000 (10%)
- Display/Retargeting: $2,000 (10%)
- Testing Budget: $1,000 (5%)

### Scaling Framework

**Month 1-2 (Testing Phase): $10K-$15K/month**
- Focus: Find what works
- Approach: Test multiple channels, messages, audiences
- Goal: Achieve <$300 CAC

**Month 3-4 (Optimization Phase): $15K-$25K/month**
- Focus: Optimize winners
- Approach: Scale winning campaigns, kill losers
- Goal: Achieve <$250 CAC

**Month 5-6 (Scale Phase): $25K-$50K/month**
- Focus: Efficient growth
- Approach: Scale proven campaigns, maintain efficiency
- Goal: Maintain <$200 CAC while growing volume

**Month 7+ (Mature Phase): $50K+/month**
- Focus: Sustainable acquisition
- Approach: Predictable, efficient scaling
- Goal: LTV:CAC > 3:1

### Budget Flexibility

**70/20/10 Rule:**
- 70% to proven, working campaigns
- 20% to optimization of existing
- 10% to testing new ideas

**ROI Thresholds:**
- If ROAS < 2:1 → Pause and optimize
- If ROAS 2-3:1 → Maintain and improve
- If ROAS > 3:1 → Scale aggressively

## Campaign Execution Plan

### Phase 1: Foundation (Month 1-2)

**Week 1-2:**
- Launch Google Search Campaigns 1+2
- Launch Meta Awareness Campaign
- Set up conversion tracking and attribution
- Install retargeting pixels

**Week 3-4:**
- Add Google Search Campaign 3
- Launch Meta Retargeting
- Launch LinkedIn Sponsored Content
- Begin building lookalike audiences

**Week 5-6:**
- Add Google Campaigns 4+5 (if budget allows)
- Launch Meta Lead Gen
- Launch LinkedIn Video Ads
- Launch Twitter Promoted Tweets

**Week 7-8:**
- Full channel activation
- First optimization based on data
- Kill underperformers
- Scale winners

---

### Phase 2: Optimization (Month 3-4)

**Focus Areas:**
- Double down on best-performing channels
- Refine audience targeting
- Improve creative based on engagement data
- Optimize landing pages for conversion
- Test pricing page variations

**Key Activities:**
- Weekly performance reviews
- Bi-weekly creative refreshes
- Monthly strategic adjustments
- Continuous A/B testing

---

### Phase 3: Scale (Month 5+)

**Growth Tactics:**
- Expand to lookalike audiences
- Increase budgets on winners
- Launch new creative variations
- Test new channels/platforms
- Explore partnership/affiliate opportunities

**Maintain:**
- CAC under target
- Creative freshness
- Channel diversification
- Testing discipline

## Creative Strategy

### Creative Themes (Rotate Every 2-3 Weeks)

**Theme 1: Cost Savings**
- "Save $14,500 vs. agency"
- Direct price comparisons
- ROI calculators
- "Professional without the price tag"

**Theme 2: Speed/Efficiency**
- "30 minutes vs. 3 months"
- "Get it done today"
- Time-to-value messaging
- Founder productivity angle

**Theme 3: Quality/Results**
- "Same frameworks consultants use"
- Customer success stories
- Before/after transformations
- "Agency-quality results"

**Theme 4: Problem/Solution**
- "Struggling to differentiate?"
- "Confused positioning costs you daily"
- Problem identification
- Clear solution path

**Theme 5: Social Proof**
- "10,000+ businesses trust Brandora"
- Founder testimonials
- Success metrics
- Community belonging

### Creative Format Mix

**Video: 40%**
- Highest engagement
- Best for storytelling
- Platform demos
- Testimonials

**Static Image: 30%**
- Easy to produce
- Good for direct response
- Test quickly
- Scale easily

**Carousel: 20%**
- Educational content
- Multi-benefit showcase
- Comparison stories
- Process explanations

**Text/Thread: 10%**
- Twitter primarily
- Thought leadership
- Quick insights
- Community engagement

### Creative Testing Cadence

**Weekly:**
- New ad copy variations
- Different hooks/CTAs
- Headline tests

**Bi-Weekly:**
- New creative concepts
- Different visual approaches
- Format variations

**Monthly:**
- Major campaign refreshes
- Seasonal themes

## Measurement & Attribution

### Primary Metrics Dashboard

**Acquisition Metrics:**
- Impressions
- Clicks
- CTR (Click-Through Rate)
- CPC (Cost Per Click)
- Website visitors
- Cost per visit

**Conversion Metrics:**
- Assessment starts
- Assessment completions
- Assessment completion rate
- Free-to-paid conversion rate
- CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)
- ROAS (Return on Ad Spend)

**Retention Metrics:**
- 30-day retention rate
- 90-day retention rate
- Upgrade rate (Starter to Pro)
- LTV (Lifetime Value)
- LTV:CAC ratio

### Attribution Model

**Multi-Touch Attribution:**
- First-touch: Initial awareness source
- Last-touch: Final conversion source
- Linear: All touchpoints weighted equally
- Time-decay: Recent touchpoints weighted higher

**Recommended:** Time-decay for B2B (longer sales cycle)

### Reporting Cadence

**Daily:**
- Spend pacing
- Critical metric alerts
- Obvious underperformers

**Weekly:**
- Performance by channel
- Creative performance
- Audience performance
- Budget utilization

**Monthly:**
- Strategic review
- ROI analysis
- Channel optimization
- Forecasting adjustments
