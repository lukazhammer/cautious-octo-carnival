## LinkedIn Document Ad Best Practices

### Document Requirements:

**Technical Specs:**
- **File Format:** PDF only
- **File Size:** 100MB maximum
- **Pages:** 2-300 pages (recommended: 8-20)
- **Dimensions:** Standard page sizes (Letter, A4)
- **Quality:** 72-150 DPI for web viewing

**Design Guidelines:**
- **Cover Page:** Compelling, branded, clear title
- **Consistency:** Branded throughout
- **Readability:** Large enough fonts (12pt+ for body)
- **Visual Appeal:** Graphics, charts, frameworks
- **Mobile-Friendly:** Legible on phone screens
- **Page Numbers:** Include for easy reference

### Content Strategy:

**Types That Perform Well:**
- How-to guides and playbooks
- Templates and frameworks
- Research reports and whitepapers
- Case studies and examples
- Industry benchmarks
- Strategic frameworks
- Workbooks and worksheets

**Content Structure:**
- **Hook (Page 1):** Compelling cover and title
- **Value (Pages 2-3):** Quick wins, key insights
- **Depth (Middle pages):** Comprehensive content
- **CTA (Last page):** Clear next step
- **Branding:** Consistent throughout

### Ad Copy Best Practices:

**Intro Text:**
- Lead with value proposition
- Explain what's in the document
- Who it's for and why they need it
- Call out key frameworks/insights
- Social proof if available
- 150-300 characters recommended

**Headline:**
- Clear and descriptive
- Benefit-focused
- Not clickbait
- Under 70 characters
- Includes key term (guide, playbook, template)

**CTA Button:**
- "Download Now"
- "Get Guide"
- "View Playbook"
- "Access Template"
- "Read Report"

### Targeting Strategy:

**Best Audiences:**
- Job titles relevant to content
- Seniority levels (varies by content)
- Industries (specific or broad)
- Company size (match content complexity)
- Skills and interests

**Avoid:**
- Overly broad targeting
- Irrelevant job functions
- Wrong seniority level
- Mismatched industries

### Performance Optimization:

**Key Metrics:**
- Document open rate: 20-40% is good
- Pages viewed: Higher = more engaging
- Average view time: Longer = better
- Click-through rate on CTA: 1-3% is solid
- Lead conversion rate: 5-15% is good

**Optimization Tactics:**
- **First Page:** Hook them immediately
- **Page 2-3:** Deliver quick value
- **Throughout:** Visual interest, not text walls
- **Last Page:** Strong, clear CTA
- **Testing:** Different documents for different audiences

### Lead Capture Integration:

**Two Approaches:**

**1. View Document → Landing Page with Form:**
- Users view full document in LinkedIn
- Click CTA to download copy
- Land on page with form
- Gate the download

**Pros:** More views, showcases value
**Cons:** Extra step to capture lead

**2. Click to Download → Form → Document:**
- User clicks to download
- Immediately see form
- Fill to access document
- Direct download

**Pros:** Direct lead capture
**Cons:** Fewer people see content first

**Recommendation:** Test both. Approach 1 usually performs better for awareness; Approach 2 for qualified leads.

### Use Cases by Funnel Stage:

**Awareness Stage:**
- Educational guides
- Industry reports
- "How to" content
- Broad appeal topics

**Consideration Stage:**
- Comparison guides
- Framework deep-dives
- ROI calculators
- Implementation guides

**Decision Stage:**
- Case studies
- Success stories
- Product guides
- Technical specifications

### Creative Refresh:

**Update Frequency:**
- Quarterly: Refresh stats and examples
- Bi-annually: Update design and layout
- Annually: Complete content overhaul

**Keep Fresh:**
- Current data and examples
- Recent case studies
- Updated screenshots
- Relevant industry trends

### Distribution Beyond LinkedIn:

**Repurpose Documents:**
- Website resource library
- Email nurture sequences
- Sales enablement assets
- Partner/affiliate distribution
- Organic social promotion
- SlideShare (LinkedIn owned)

**Maximize ROI:**
- One document, multiple uses
- Drive traffic from multiple sources
- Build comprehensive resource library
- Establish thought leadership

## A/B Testing Framework

### Test Variables:

**1. Document Type:**
- Guide vs. Playbook vs. Template
- Theoretical vs. Practical
- Long (20+ pages) vs. Short (8-10 pages)

**2. Cover Design:**
- Minimalist vs. Visual
- Professional vs. Approachable
- Different color schemes
- Image vs. text-focused

**3. Title/Headline:**
- How-to vs. Ultimate guide
- Problem-focused vs. Solution-focused
- Specific vs. Broad

**4. Ad Copy:**
- Long (300 chars) vs. Short (150 chars)
- Feature vs. Benefit
- Question vs. Statement

**5. CTA:**
- Different button text
- Soft vs. Direct
- Action words variation

### Success Metrics:

**Primary:**
- Lead conversion rate
- Cost per lead
- Document engagement (pages viewed)

**Secondary:**
- Open rate
- Click-through rate
- Download rate
- Sales qualified leads

**Tertiary:**
- Time spent viewing
- Return visits
- Social shares
- Email opt-in rate
