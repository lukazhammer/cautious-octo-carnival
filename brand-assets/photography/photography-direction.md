# Photography Direction & Guidelines

## Photography Style

### Overall Aesthetic

**Brandora photography should feel:**
- Professional yet approachable
- Authentic, not overly staged
- Diverse and inclusive
- Energetic but focused
- Strategic and intentional

**Visual characteristics:**
- Natural lighting preferred
- Bright and airy
- Clean, uncluttered backgrounds
- Genuine moments over posed shots
- Mix of close-ups and environmental shots

---

## Color Treatment

### Color Palette Integration

**Primary approach:** Complement brand colors without forcing them

**Color guidelines:**
- Favor cool tones (blues, grays) that align with brand palette
- Warm accents (yellows, golds) for energy
- Avoid overly saturated colors
- Maintain color consistency across photo sets
- Navy and blue clothing can reinforce brand

**Post-processing:**
- Slight blue tone in highlights
- Maintain natural skin tones
- Increase clarity moderately
- Boost shadows slightly for airy feel
- Avoid heavy filters or artificial looks

## Composition Guidelines

### Rule of Thirds
- Place key subjects at intersection points
- Don't center everything
- Create visual interest and balance

### Framing
- Use environmental elements to frame subjects
- Doorways, windows, architectural elements
- Create depth and context

### Depth of Field
- Shallow DOF for portraits and details
- Wider DOF for environmental shots
- Blurred backgrounds reduce distraction
- Keep subject sharp and clear

### Perspective
- Eye-level for authenticity
- Slightly elevated for overview shots
- Avoid extreme angles
- Mix of wide, medium, and close shots

---

## Lighting Guidelines

### Natural Light (Preferred)
- Soft, diffused window light
- Avoid harsh direct sunlight
- Golden hour for warm, flattering light
- Overcast days for even, soft light
- Position subjects facing light source

### Artificial Light (When Needed)
- Soft, diffused lighting
- Avoid harsh overhead fluorescents
- Use LED panels or softboxes
- Maintain natural color temperature
- Avoid mixed lighting (natural + artificial)

### Exposure
- Bright and airy overall feel
- Slightly overexposed is better than underexposed
- Maintain detail in highlights
- Lift shadows in post-processing
- Consistent exposure across photo sets

## Diversity & Inclusion

### Representation Guidelines

**Gender:**
- Balance male, female, non-binary representation
- Avoid gender stereotypes
- Represent all genders in leadership roles

**Ethnicity:**
- Reflect global diversity
- Avoid tokenization
- Natural, authentic representation
- Mixed ethnicity in group shots

**Age:**
- Range from late 20s to 50s
- Represent experience levels
- Avoid ageism stereotypes

**Ability:**
- Include people with visible and invisible disabilities
- Show assistive technology naturally
- Wheelchair users, hearing aids, etc.
- Not always the focus—natural inclusion

**Body types:**
- Diverse body sizes and shapes
- Avoid stereotypical "executive" look
- Real people, not just models

**Cultural:**
- Diverse cultural backgrounds
- Avoid cultural stereotypes
- Global representation

## Image Specifications

### File Requirements

**Format:**
- Deliver: JPG (final) + RAW files
- Web: JPG optimized for web
- Print: High-res TIFF or JPG

**Resolution:**
- Web: Minimum 2400px on longest side
- Print: 300 DPI at intended size
- Hero images: 4000px+ width

**Aspect Ratios:**
- 16:9 (website heroes, presentations)
- 4:3 (general use, presentations)
- 1:1 (social media, Instagram)
- 9:16 (vertical, stories, mobile)
- 2:3 (portrait orientation)

**Color Space:**
- Web: sRGB
- Print: Adobe RGB or CMYK

---

## Post-Processing Guidelines

### Basic Adjustments
- **Exposure:** Bright, airy (+0.3 to +0.7)
- **Contrast:** Moderate (+10 to +20)
- **Highlights:** Slightly reduced (-10 to -20)
- **Shadows:** Lifted (+20 to +40)
- **Clarity:** Moderate increase (+10 to +20)
- **Vibrance:** Slight boost (+5 to +15)

### Color Grading
- **Temperature:** Slightly cool or neutral
- **Tint:** Minimal adjustment
- **HSL:** Shift blues toward brand blue
- **Skin tones:** Natural, even
- **Consistency:** Match across sets

### Sharpening
- **Amount:** Moderate (40-60)
- **Radius:** 1.0-1.5
- **Detail:** 25-50
- **Masking:** Apply to subjects, not backgrounds

### Avoid
- Over-processing
- Heavy vignettes
- Artificial bokeh
- HDR effects
- Heavy Instagram filters
- Unnatural skin smoothing

## Stock Photography Guidelines

When using stock photos (vs. custom photography):

### Where to Source
- Unsplash (free, high quality)
- Pexels (free)
- Adobe Stock (paid, curated)
- Getty Images (paid, premium)

### Selection Criteria
- Matches style guidelines above
- High resolution (2400px+ minimum)
- Natural, authentic moments
- Diverse representation
- Not obviously staged or stock-looking
- Proper licensing for commercial use

### Red Flags to Avoid
- Overly perfect, unrealistic scenarios
- Dated technology or clothing
- Obvious watermarks or branding
- Overused/cliché stock images
- Forced diversity (tokenism)
- Unnatural color processing

## User-Generated Content

### When to Use UGC
- Testimonials and case studies
- Customer success stories
- Community highlights
- Social proof
- Behind-the-scenes content

### Quality Standards
- Minimum resolution standards maintained
- Clear, well-lit images
- Represents brand values
- Permission and rights obtained
- Proper attribution given

### Don'ts
- Don't use low-quality images
- Don't use unflattering photos
- Don't misrepresent context
- Don't use without permission
- Don't over-edit to point of misrepresentation

## Image Treatments

### Overlays for Text
When placing text over images:

**Darkening overlay:**
- 30-50% black or navy overlay
- Gradient from transparent to dark
- Ensures text legibility

**Lightening overlay:**
- 50-70% white overlay for light text
- Blur image slightly
- Reduce saturation

**Color overlay:**
- Brand colors at 80-90% opacity
- Navy or blue tint
- Subtle, not overpowering

### Duotone Effect
- Navy (#070058) + Blue (#6F8AEC)
- Navy (#070058) + Yellow (#FFBE44)
- Use sparingly, for special treatments
- Maintain recognizability

## Legal & Rights

### Model Releases
- Required for all identifiable people
- Stored securely with image files
- Specify usage rights clearly
- Cover commercial use explicitly

### Property Releases
- Required for identifiable private property
- Logos and brands require permission
- Trademarks must be released or removed

### Usage Rights
- Specify: commercial, editorial, duration
- Track licensing for stock images
- Maintain attribution when required
- Respect exclusive rights when purchased
