# Brand Voice & Writing Guidelines

## Voice Principles

### Our Voice Is...

#### 1. Clear Over Clever
We value understanding over wordplay. Our audience is busy‚Äîrespect their time with direct, jargon-free communication.

**Instead of this:** "Leverage our synergistic platform to actualize your brand's paradigmatic potential."
**Write this:** "Use Brandora to discover what makes your brand unique and how to communicate it clearly."

---

#### 2. Empowering Over Prescriptive
We guide and enable, we don't dictate or lecture. Position users as capable decision-makers.

**Instead of this:** "You must complete all assessment sections to get results."
**Write this:** "Complete the assessment to unlock your personalized brand strategy."

---

#### 3. Conversational But Professional
We're the expert colleague, not the distant consultant or overly casual friend.

**Instead of this:** "Hey there! OMG, your brand is gonna be lit! üî•"
**Write this:** "Welcome! We're excited to help you build a brand strategy that resonates with your ideal customers."

---

#### 4. Confident Without Arrogance
We know our expertise but acknowledge the complexity of business challenges.

**Instead of this:** "Our AI perfectly solves all your branding problems instantly."
**Write this:** "Our AI-powered platform guides you through proven brand strategy frameworks, helping you make informed positioning decisions."

---

#### 5. Data-Informed and Human
We balance analytical insights with empathy for the human experience.

**Instead of this:** "User cohort engagement metrics indicate suboptimal conversion trajectory."
**Write this:** "Your current positioning isn't connecting with your target audience. Here's how to improve it."

---

## Writing Style

### Sentence Structure

**Keep sentences concise:**
- **Target:** 15-20 words per sentence
- **Maximum:** 30 words
- **Vary length** for rhythm and readability

**Good example:**
"Brandora helps you discover your unique market position. We guide you through research, positioning, and messaging‚Äîgiving you the clarity to compete with confidence."

**Too long:**
"Brandora is a platform that helps entrepreneurs and marketing professionals discover their unique market position by guiding them through a comprehensive process of research, positioning, and messaging development, ultimately giving them the clarity they need to compete with confidence in their respective markets."

---

### Active Voice Over Passive

**Passive voice** makes writing feel distant and weak.
**Active voice** is direct and engaging.

| Passive (Weak) | Active (Strong) |
|----------------|----------------|
| Your brand assessment will be analyzed by our AI. | Our AI analyzes your brand assessment. |
| Recommendations are generated based on your inputs. | We generate recommendations based on your inputs. |
| Results can be exported as a PDF. | Export your results as a PDF. |
| The platform was designed to be intuitive. | We designed the platform to be intuitive. |

**When passive is okay:**
- When the actor is unknown or unimportant
- When you want to emphasize the object, not the subject
- In technical documentation where it's conventional

### Humor and Emotion

**When to use humor:**

### Inclusive Language

**Use:**
- ‚úì They/them/their as singular pronouns
- ‚úì Folks, people, everyone (not "guys")
- ‚úì Business owner, entrepreneur, professional
- ‚úì Partner, spouse (not "wife/husband" unless specified)

## Grammar and Mechanics

### Capitalization

**Brand Names:**
- Brandora (always capitalized, never all caps except in logos)
- Brand Assessment (capitalize product names)
- Pro Tier, Premium Tier, Enterprise Tier (capitalize tier names)

**Headings and Titles:**
- Use sentence case for most headings
- Example: "How to define your target audience"
- Not: "How To Define Your Target Audience"

**In UI:**
- Buttons: Sentence case ("Start free trial")
- Menu items: Sentence case ("Account settings")
- Form labels: Sentence case ("Email address")

**Exception:** All caps for overlines/eyebrows (sparingly)

---

### Punctuation

**Commas:**
- Use Oxford comma (serial comma)
  - Correct: "Research, positioning, and messaging"
  - Incorrect: "Research, positioning and messaging"

**Periods:**
- Use periods for complete sentences
- Skip periods for headlines, buttons, labels
- Use one space after a period, not two

**Dashes:**
- Em dash (‚Äî) for breaks in thought: "We help entrepreneurs‚Äîfrom solopreneurs to startups‚Äîbuild strong brands."
- En dash (‚Äì) for ranges: "2024‚Äì2025"
- Hyphen (-) for compound modifiers: "data-driven strategy"

**Ellipses:**
- Use sparingly
- Three dots with spaces: ". . ."
- Better: rephrase to eliminate

**Exclamation Points:**
- Use sparingly! (see what we did there?)
- Maximum one per page of content
- Never use multiple (!!!)
- Okay for success messages and celebrations

**Question Marks:**
- Use for direct questions
- Avoid in headlines unless truly asking a question

---

### Number Formatting

**Numbers:**
- Spell out one through nine
- Use numerals for 10 and above
- Exception: When used with a unit or in data
  - "3 minutes"
  - "5 steps"
  - "7-day trial"

**Large Numbers:**
- 1,000+ ‚Üí Use comma separators (1,234)
- 10,000+ ‚Üí Can use 10K in casual contexts
- 1,000,000+ ‚Üí Can use 1M in casual contexts
- In formal contexts, write out or use commas (1,000,000)

**Percentages:**
- Use numeral + % symbol: "50% increase"
- Not: "fifty percent increase"

**Currency:**
- Use $ symbol: "$99/month"
- For clarity: "USD $99" in international contexts
- Range: "$99-$199" or "$99 to $199"

**Dates:**
- Month DD, YYYY ‚Üí January 15, 2025
- Avoid MM/DD/YYYY (confusing internationally)
- In UI: "Jan 15, 2025" (abbreviated month is okay)

**Time:**
- Use 12-hour format: "2:00 PM EST"
- Include time zone when relevant

---

### Acronyms and Abbreviations

**First Use:**
- Spell out + acronym: "Artificial Intelligence (AI)"
- Subsequent uses: Just the acronym "AI"

**Common Acronyms (Don't Need to Spell Out):**
- AI, API, B2B, B2C, CEO, CTA, FAQ, PDF, ROI, SaaS, SEO, URL

**Less Common (Spell Out First Time):**
- SWOT, GTM, ICP, USP, TAM, CAC, LTV

**Brand-Specific:**
- BA (Brand Assessment) - spell out first time
- MP (Messaging Pillars) - spell out first time

---

### Contractions

**Use contractions** to sound conversational and approachable:
- We're (we are)
- You're (you are)
- It's (it is)
- Don't (do not)
- Can't (cannot)
- Won't (will not)
- Shouldn't (should not)

**Avoid contractions** in:
- Legal content
- Very formal documentation
- When you want to emphasize: "Do not share your password" (stronger than "Don't share")

## Common Writing Scenarios

### Headlines

**Best practices:**
- Front-load value
- Be specific, not generic
- Use numbers when relevant
- Make a promise you can keep

**Generic:** "Improve Your Brand"
**Better:** "Define Your Brand Position in 3 Steps"

**Generic:** "Marketing Made Easy"
**Better:** "Cut Your Strategy Time in Half with AI-Powered Frameworks"

---

### CTAs (Calls-to-Action)

**Best practices:**
- Start with a verb
- Be specific about outcome
- Create urgency without false scarcity
- Reduce friction

**Weak:** "Submit" / "Click here" / "Learn more"
**Strong:**
- "Start free assessment"
- "Get my brand strategy"
- "See pricing plans"
- "Download the guide"
- "Try Brandora free"

---

### Error Messages

**Best practices:**
- Explain what happened
- Provide a solution
- Use plain language
- Don't blame the user

**Bad:** "Error 403: Forbidden"
**Good:** "You don't have access to this feature. Upgrade to Pro to unlock competitor analysis."

**Bad:** "Invalid input"
**Good:** "Email address should include an @ symbol. Try again?"

---

### Success Messages

**Best practices:**
- Confirm what happened
- Suggest logical next step
- Celebrate when appropriate

**Basic:** "Saved"
**Better:** "Changes saved! Your brand assessment is updated."

**Basic:** "Subscription created"
**Better:** "Welcome to Pro! Your account is upgraded and all features are now unlocked."

## Word Choice

### Words to Use Often
- You / your (user-focused)
- Discover (not "find")
- Build (not "create" or "make")
- Transform (significant change)
- Clarity (core value)
- Strategic (intentional, thoughtful)
- Unique (one-of-a-kind)
- Professional (high quality)
- Effective (results-driven)

### Words to Use Sparingly
- Amazing, awesome, incredible (overused)
- Game-changing, revolutionary (hyperbolic)
- Easy, simple (may invalidate user struggles)
- Just (minimizes importance)
- Obviously, clearly (can feel condescending)

### Words to Avoid
- Synergy
- Paradigm
- Leverage (as verb)
- Utilize (use "use")
- Solutions (overused, vague)
- Cutting-edge (clich√©)
- World-class (meaningless)
- Best-in-class (prove it instead)

## Content Checklist

Before publishing any content, verify:

**Clarity:**
- [ ] Is the main point clear?
- [ ] Could a 10th grader understand it?
- [ ] Are there any unnecessary words?

**Brand Alignment:**
- [ ] Does it sound like Brandora?
- [ ] Does it reflect our personality traits?
- [ ] Is the tone appropriate for context?

**User Focus:**
- [ ] Is it valuable to the reader?
- [ ] Does it address their needs/pain points?
- [ ] Does it empower them to take action?

**Accuracy:**
- [ ] Are all facts correct?
- [ ] Are claims backed by evidence?
- [ ] Is pricing/feature information current?

**Mechanics:**
- [ ] Grammar and spelling correct?
- [ ] Follows style guidelines?
- [ ] Links work properly?
- [ ] Formatted correctly?
