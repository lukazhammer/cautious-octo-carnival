# Customer Persona Development Prompts

## Persona Creation (25 prompts)

### Foundation Building

1. **Complete Persona Creation**
   ```
   Create a detailed customer persona for:

   Product/Service: [your offering]
   Target demographic: [age, location, income, education, occupation]
   Primary problem solved: [main pain point]
   Industry context: [relevant industry details]

   Include:
   - Demographic profile
   - Psychographic traits (values, attitudes, interests)
   - Goals and motivations
   - Pain points and challenges
   - Buying behavior and decision process
   - Preferred channels and content
   - Objections and concerns
   - Day in the life scenario
   ```

2. **B2B Persona Development**
   ```
   Create a B2B buyer persona for:

   Solution: [what you sell]
   Target company size: [employees, revenue]
   Industry: [specific industry]
   Job title/role: [decision maker or influencer]

   Include:
   - Professional demographics
   - Business challenges and KPIs
   - Decision-making authority and process
   - Budget and procurement requirements
   - Information sources and influences
   - Success metrics they care about
   - Career goals and motivations
   - Typical buying journey
   ```

3. **Psychographic Deep Dive**
   ```
   What psychographic traits define [target audience description]?

   Analyze:
   - Core values and beliefs
   - Lifestyle preferences
   - Personality characteristics
   - Attitudes toward [relevant topics]
   - Aspirations and fears
   - Social identity and affiliations
   - Decision-making style
   - Brand affinities

   Go beyond demographics to understand mindset.
   ```

4. **Jobs-to-be-Done Persona**
   ```
   Create a persona based on the "job" customers hire our product to do:

   Functional job: [task to accomplish]
   Emotional job: [how they want to feel]
   Social job: [how they want to be perceived]
   Context: [when/where this job arises]

   Profile the person who has this job and describe:
   - Current solutions they use
   - Why current solutions fail
   - Success criteria for the job
   - Constraints and considerations
   ```

5. **Life Stage Persona**
   ```
   Develop a persona at this specific life stage:

   Life stage: [recent graduate, new parent, retiree, etc.]
   Relevant transition: [what's changing in their life]
   Product category: [what you offer]

   How does this life stage affect:
   - Priorities and values
   - Available resources (time, money, attention)
   - Decision criteria
   - Pain points unique to this stage
   - Information needs
   - Buying patterns
   ```

### Persona Validation & Research

6. **Persona Interview Questions**
   ```
   Generate interview questions to validate this persona: [persona description]

   Create questions that uncover:
   - Background and context
   - Current challenges and frustrations
   - Goals and desired outcomes
   - Current solutions and workarounds
   - Decision-making process
   - Objections and concerns
   - Ideal solution characteristics

   Include 15-20 open-ended questions organized by topic.
   ```

7. **Persona Assumption Testing**
   ```
   I've made these assumptions about my target customer:
   - [assumption 1]
   - [assumption 2]
   - [assumption 3]

   For each assumption:
   - Is it testable? How?
   - What evidence would validate it?
   - What questions should I ask?
   - What data should I collect?
   - How risky is this assumption if wrong?
   ```

8. **Buying Triggers Analysis**
   ```
   Identify buying triggers for [persona description] when considering [product type]:

   What events or circumstances trigger them to:
   - Recognize they have a problem
   - Start looking for solutions
   - Evaluate specific options
   - Make a purchase decision
   - Become ready to buy NOW vs. later

   Map triggers to each stage of awareness and buying journey.
   ```

9. **Daily Challenges Mapping**
   ```
   What are the daily challenges of [persona] in [context]?

   Map a typical day:
   - Morning priorities and challenges
   - Workday pain points and frustrations
   - Evening concerns and tasks
   - Weekly/monthly recurring issues

   Which of these challenges does our [product/service] address?
   How do we fit into their daily life?
   ```

10. **Persona Objection Analysis**
    ```
    For this persona: [description]
    Considering this offer: [your product/service and price]

    What are their likely objections:
    - Rational objections (price, features, fit)
    - Emotional objections (fear, doubt, distrust)
    - Social objections (what others might think)
    - Practical objections (time, effort, change)

    For each objection, suggest:
    - Root cause
    - How to prevent it
    - How to overcome it
    - Messaging to address it
    ```

### Persona Segmentation

11. **Multi-Persona Development**
    ```
    I serve multiple customer types for [product/service].

    Create 3-5 distinct personas representing:
    - Different use cases or needs
    - Different decision-making authority
    - Different company sizes or industries
    - Different experience levels or sophistication

    For each persona, highlight:
    - Unique characteristics
    - Distinct needs or goals
    - Different messaging angles
    - Separate value propositions
    ```

12. **Primary vs. Secondary Personas**
    ```
    I have these potential personas: [list]

    Help me determine:
    - Which is the primary persona (most valuable, best fit)
    - Which are secondary (still important but different priority)
    - Which are tertiary (nice to have but not core)

    Criteria:
    - Market size and accessibility
    - Willingness and ability to pay
    - Fit with our capabilities
    - Strategic importance
    - Resource requirements to serve
    ```

13. **Decision-Maker vs. Influencer Personas**
    ```
    For B2B sales of [product/service]:

    Create separate personas for:
    - Economic buyer (holds budget, final decision)
    - Champion (internal advocate)
    - End user (will use the product)
    - Blocker (might resist or veto)

    For each role:
    - What they care about
    - What success looks like to them
    - How to message to them
    - Their role in the buying process
    ```

14. **Persona Evolution Stages**
    ```
    Create personas representing different stages of customer evolution:

    Beginner: [just starting, learning basics]
    Intermediate: [has some experience, growing]
    Advanced: [experienced, sophisticated needs]
    Expert: [very experienced, specialized needs]

    For [product category] in [industry], describe:
    - Different needs at each stage
    - Different language they use
    - Different priorities
    - Different buying criteria
    - How to serve each stage
    ```

15. **Persona Prioritization Matrix**
    ```
    I have these potential persona segments: [list]

    Evaluate each on:
    - Market size (how many)
    - Revenue potential (value per customer)
    - Acquisition cost (cost to reach and convert)
    - Product fit (how well we serve them)
    - Competition (how saturated)
    - Strategic value (long-term importance)

    Recommend prioritization and resource allocation.
    ```

### Persona Application

16. **Persona-Based Messaging**
    ```
    For this persona: [detailed description]

    Create messaging that resonates:
    - Headline that captures attention
    - Value proposition that speaks to them
    - Pain points to emphasize
    - Benefits that matter most
    - Language and tone to use
    - Metaphors or analogies that work
    - Social proof that's relevant
    - Call-to-action that motivates
    ```

17. **Content Preferences Analysis**
    ```
    For persona: [description]

    What content formats, topics, and channels work best?

    Determine:
    - Preferred content formats (video, written, audio, visual)
    - Content topics of interest
    - Information depth preference (overview vs. detailed)
    - Tone and style (formal, casual, technical, simple)
    - Consumption context (mobile, desktop, on-the-go)
    - Sharing behavior
    - Engagement patterns
    ```

18. **Channel Preferences**
    ```
    Where does [persona] spend time and how do they prefer to be reached?

    Map their presence and behavior on:
    - Social media platforms
    - Professional networks
    - Content platforms (blogs, YouTube, podcasts)
    - Email engagement
    - Search behavior
    - Offline channels
    - Community and forums

    Prioritize channels for marketing efforts.
    ```

19. **Customer Journey Mapping**
    ```
    Map the complete customer journey for [persona]:

    Stage 1 - Unaware: [don't know problem exists]
    Stage 2 - Problem Aware: [recognize problem, seeking education]
    Stage 3 - Solution Aware: [know solutions exist, researching options]
    Stage 4 - Product Aware: [evaluating specific products/vendors]
    Stage 5 - Most Aware: [comparing final options, ready to buy]

    For each stage, identify:
    - Questions they're asking
    - Information they need
    - Content that helps
    - Emotions they're experiencing
    - Actions to encourage
    ```

20. **Persona Empathy Map**
    ```
    Create an empathy map for [persona]:

    Think/Feel:
    - What are their worries and aspirations?
    - What truly matters to them?

    See:
    - What do they observe in their environment?
    - What influences are they exposed to?

    Hear:
    - What are others saying to them?
    - Who influences their opinions?

    Say/Do:
    - What do they say in public?
    - How do their words and actions differ?

    Pains:
    - What frustrates them?
    - What obstacles do they face?

    Gains:
    - What do they want to achieve?
    - What would success look like?
    ```

### Advanced Persona Work

21. **Persona Conflicts and Tensions**
    ```
    For persona [description], identify internal conflicts:

    What tensions exist between:
    - What they want vs. what they can afford
    - Ideal solution vs. practical constraints
    - Personal preference vs. company requirements
    - Short-term needs vs. long-term goals
    - Risk aversion vs. desire for innovation

    How do these tensions affect:
    - Decision-making process
    - Buying criteria and priorities
    - Objections and hesitations
    - Need for reassurance
    ```

22. **Persona Negative Example**
    ```
    Create a negative persona (who we DON'T want to serve):

    Characteristics:
    - Wrong fit indicators
    - Red flags in behavior or needs
    - Unprofitable customer traits
    - High maintenance, low value
    - Wrong expectations or goals

    Why they're wrong for us:
    - Product/service isn't right for them
    - They won't get value
    - Costly to serve
    - Won't succeed or be satisfied

    How to qualify them out early.
    ```

23. **Persona Value Perception**
    ```
    How does [persona] perceive and evaluate value?

    Analyze:
    - What "value" means to them specifically
    - ROI calculation method (if B2B)
    - Tangible vs. intangible benefits weighting
    - Short-term vs. long-term value trade-offs
    - Price sensitivity and budget reality
    - Comparison to alternatives
    - Willingness to pay premium (for what?)

    How to demonstrate value in their terms.
    ```

24. **Persona Information Sources**
    ```
    Where does [persona] go for information when researching [category]?

    Map their research process:
    - Initial awareness/education sources
    - Trusted experts and influencers
    - Peer recommendations and communities
    - Professional sources (if B2B)
    - Review sites and comparison tools
    - Search behavior and keywords
    - Information consumption pattern

    Where should we have presence and visibility?
    ```

25. **Persona Success Definition**
    ```
    What does success look like to [persona] after using our [product/service]?

    Define success as:
    - Immediate wins (quick results)
    - Short-term outcomes (weeks/months)
    - Long-term transformation (sustainable change)
    - Tangible metrics (measurable results)
    - Intangible benefits (how they feel)
    - Social proof (what they can tell others)
    - Career/business impact (professional gains)

    How to design for and deliver on this success definition.
    ```

## How to Use Persona Prompts

### Research Phase
1. Start with foundation prompts (1-5) to draft initial personas
2. Use validation prompts (6-10) to test assumptions
3. Refine based on real customer conversations

### Segmentation Phase
1. Use segmentation prompts (11-15) to identify distinct groups
2. Prioritize based on strategic value
3. Create detailed profiles for top 2-3 personas

### Application Phase
1. Use application prompts (16-20) to operationalize personas
2. Create persona-specific marketing assets
3. Map journeys and touchpoints

### Optimization Phase
1. Use advanced prompts (21-25) to deepen understanding
2. Identify gaps and opportunities
3. Continuously refine based on data

## Best Practices

1. **Base on Real Data**: Interview actual customers, use analytics, validate assumptions
2. **Make Them Specific**: Generic personas aren't useful - get detailed
3. **Give Them Names**: Make personas feel like real people
4. **Include Quotes**: Use actual language from customer interviews
5. **Update Regularly**: Personas evolve as markets and customers change
6. **Share Widely**: Everyone should know and use personas
7. **Test Everything**: Use personas to evaluate decisions ("Would Sarah value this feature?")
8. **Focus on Jobs**: What are they trying to accomplish?
9. **Understand Context**: When and why do they need your solution?
10. **Map the Journey**: Don't just describe them - show how they buy

## Quick-Start Workflow

1. **Draft** (30 min): Use prompts 1-3 to create initial persona
2. **Validate** (1 week): Use prompt 6 to create interview guide, talk to 5-10 real customers
3. **Refine** (1 hour): Update persona based on interviews
4. **Apply** (ongoing): Use prompts 16-20 to create persona-specific marketing
5. **Optimize** (quarterly): Use prompts 21-25 to deepen and evolve
