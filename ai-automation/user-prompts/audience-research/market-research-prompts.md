# Market Research Prompts

## Market Analysis (25 prompts)

### Market Size & Opportunity

1. **Total Addressable Market (TAM) Analysis**
   ```
   Analyze the total addressable market for [product/service] in [geographic region]:

   Calculate or estimate:
   - Total potential customers (market size)
   - Average revenue per customer
   - Total market value (TAM)
   - Market growth rate (historical and projected)
   - Market maturity stage

   Provide:
   - Data sources and methodology
   - Key assumptions
   - Confidence level in estimates
   - Comparison to adjacent markets
   ```

2. **Serviceable Market Analysis**
   ```
   Given our capabilities and positioning, what's our realistic serviceable market?

   Total market: [TAM estimate]
   Our constraints:
   - Geographic reach: [where we can serve]
   - Customer segments we serve: [specific niches]
   - Product limitations: [what we can/can't do]
   - Resource constraints: [capacity limits]

   Calculate:
   - Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM)
   - Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM)
   - Realistic penetration rate
   - Revenue potential over 1, 3, 5 years
   ```

3. **Market Growth Trends**
   ```
   What are the growth trends in [industry/market]?

   Analyze:
   - Historical growth (past 5-10 years)
   - Current growth rate
   - Projected future growth
   - Growth drivers and accelerators
   - Growth inhibitors and risks
   - Regional variations in growth
   - Segment-specific growth rates

   Identify fastest-growing segments and opportunities.
   ```

4. **Emerging Market Opportunities**
   ```
   Identify emerging opportunities in [industry]:

   Look for:
   - Underserved customer segments
   - New use cases or applications
   - Technology-enabled possibilities
   - Regulatory or policy changes creating opportunity
   - Demographic shifts opening new markets
   - Geographic expansion opportunities
   - White space in competitive landscape

   Evaluate each opportunity by size, timing, and fit.
   ```

5. **Market Saturation Analysis**
   ```
   How saturated is the [specific market] in [region]?

   Assess:
   - Number of competitors
   - Market concentration (few large players vs. fragmented)
   - Customer acquisition difficulty
   - Price pressure and commoditization
   - Innovation rate
   - Barriers to entry
   - Available market share to capture

   Is this market still attractive or should we look elsewhere?
   ```

### Industry Analysis

6. **Industry Structure Analysis (Porter's Five Forces)**
   ```
   Analyze [industry] using Porter's Five Forces:

   1. Competitive Rivalry:
      - Number and strength of competitors
      - Market growth rate
      - Industry concentration
      - Differentiation level
      - Switching costs
      - Exit barriers

   2. Threat of New Entrants:
      - Barriers to entry
      - Capital requirements
      - Economies of scale
      - Regulatory requirements

   3. Bargaining Power of Suppliers:
      - Supplier concentration
      - Uniqueness of inputs
      - Switching costs

   4. Bargaining Power of Buyers:
      - Buyer concentration
      - Price sensitivity
      - Product differentiation
      - Switching costs

   5. Threat of Substitutes:
      - Alternative solutions
      - Relative price-performance
      - Switching costs

   Overall industry attractiveness and profit potential?
   ```

7. **Industry Trends and Disruptions**
   ```
   What trends are reshaping [industry]?

   Identify trends in:
   - Technology (automation, AI, platforms, etc.)
   - Consumer behavior (preferences, expectations)
   - Business models (subscription, marketplace, etc.)
   - Regulation and policy
   - Globalization or localization
   - Sustainability and social responsibility

   For each trend:
   - Current impact
   - Future trajectory
   - Opportunity or threat for us?
   - Required response or adaptation
   ```

8. **Industry Value Chain Analysis**
   ```
   Map the value chain for [industry]:

   From supplier to end customer:
   - Raw materials/inputs
   - Production/creation
   - Distribution
   - Marketing/sales
   - Service/support

   Analyze:
   - Where is value created?
   - Where is margin captured?
   - Who has power in the chain?
   - Where are inefficiencies?
   - Where can we play?
   - Potential for disintermediation?
   ```

9. **Industry Profitability Analysis**
   ```
   What's the profitability profile of [industry]?

   Research:
   - Average industry margins (gross, operating, net)
   - Variance between top and bottom performers
   - What drives profitability differences?
   - Industry economics (fixed vs. variable costs)
   - Revenue models and pricing power
   - Investment requirements and returns

   Benchmark against other industries.
   ```

10. **Regulatory and Compliance Landscape**
    ```
    What regulatory factors affect [industry] in [region]?

    Map:
    - Current regulations and compliance requirements
    - Regulatory bodies and oversight
    - Pending legislation or policy changes
    - Industry self-regulation and standards
    - International regulatory differences
    - Compliance costs and barriers
    - Recent enforcement actions or trends

    Impact on business model and operations?
    ```

### Customer & Demand Analysis

11. **Customer Demographics Research**
    ```
    What demographic data is available for [target audience] in [region]?

    Gather data on:
    - Age distribution
    - Gender breakdown
    - Income levels
    - Education levels
    - Occupation types
    - Geographic concentration
    - Household composition
    - Ethnicity/cultural background

    Sources: Census, industry reports, market research firms.
    Where can we access this data?
    ```

12. **Consumer Behavior Analysis**
    ```
    Compare consumer behavior in [Market A] vs. [Market B]:

    Analyze differences in:
    - Purchase frequency and patterns
    - Average transaction size
    - Channel preferences (online vs. offline)
    - Brand loyalty levels
    - Price sensitivity
    - Decision-making process
    - Influencing factors
    - Seasonal variations

    Implications for market entry or expansion strategy?
    ```

13. **Demand Drivers Analysis**
    ```
    What drives demand for [product/service] in [market]?

    Identify:
    - Primary demand drivers (what creates need)
    - Seasonal factors
    - Economic factors (GDP, employment, income)
    - Demographic factors (population, age, urbanization)
    - Social/cultural factors
    - Technology factors
    - Regulatory factors

    Which drivers are growing vs. declining?
    ```

14. **Customer Segmentation Research**
    ```
    How is [market] segmented by existing players?

    Analyze segmentation by:
    - Demographics (age, income, location)
    - Psychographics (lifestyle, values)
    - Behavior (usage, loyalty, price sensitivity)
    - Needs (problems to solve)
    - Geography (regions, urban/rural)

    Which segments are:
    - Largest by size
    - Most profitable
    - Fastest growing
    - Underserved
    ```

15. **Buyer Decision Process Research**
    ```
    How do customers in [market] make purchase decisions for [category]?

    Research:
    - Average decision timeline
    - Information sources consulted
    - Evaluation criteria and priorities
    - Who's involved in decision (individual vs. group)
    - Triggers that initiate buying process
    - Barriers that delay or prevent purchase
    - Post-purchase evaluation process

    Map the typical buyer journey with timelines.
    ```

### Competitive Market Research

16. **Market Share Analysis**
    ```
    What's the market share distribution in [market/category]?

    Research:
    - Top 5-10 players by market share
    - Market concentration (Herfindahl index)
    - Share trends (growing/declining players)
    - Regional variations in share
    - Share by customer segment
    - Share by product category

    Is market dominated by few or fragmented?
    ```

17. **Competitive Set Identification**
    ```
    Who are all the competitors in [market] for [solution type]?

    Categorize:
    - Direct competitors (same solution, same audience)
    - Indirect competitors (different solution, same problem)
    - Substitute products (alternative ways to solve problem)
    - Emerging competitors (startups, new entrants)
    - Adjacent competitors (could expand into our space)

    Map by threat level and market position.
    ```

18. **Pricing Analysis Across Market**
    ```
    What's the pricing landscape in [market] for [product type]?

    Research:
    - Price range (low to high)
    - Price clustering (common price points)
    - Pricing models (one-time, subscription, usage-based)
    - Price-value positioning
    - Discounting practices
    - Price trends over time

    Where are gaps in pricing tiers?
    What does pricing tell us about positioning?
    ```

19. **Market Entry Timing Analysis**
    ```
    Is now the right time to enter [market]?

    Evaluate:
    - Market maturity (emerging, growth, mature, declining)
    - Current competitive intensity
    - Customer readiness for new solutions
    - Technology readiness
    - Regulatory environment
    - Economic conditions
    - Investment climate

    What's the optimal entry timing and why?
    ```

20. **Geographic Market Comparison**
    ```
    Compare [market/industry] across different geographies:

    Regions to compare: [list regions/countries]

    Analyze for each:
    - Market size and growth
    - Competitive intensity
    - Customer characteristics
    - Pricing levels
    - Distribution channels
    - Regulatory environment
    - Cultural factors
    - Market maturity

    Rank regions by attractiveness for entry/expansion.
    ```

### Advanced Market Research

21. **Market Adjacencies Exploration**
    ```
    What adjacent markets could we expand into from [current market]?

    For each adjacency:
    - Market size and growth
    - Entry barriers
    - Strategic fit
    - Resource requirements
    - Risk level
    ```

    ```
    - Market size and growth
    - Competitive landscape changes
    - Technology disruptions
    - Customer behavior shifts
    - Regulatory changes
    - Economic conditions

    Strategic implications of each scenario?
    ```

## How to Conduct Market Research

### Research Process

1. **Define Objectives** (What do you need to know and why?)
2. **Identify Sources** (Where will you find information?)
3. **Gather Data** (Execute research using multiple methods)
4. **Analyze Findings** (Identify patterns and insights)
5. **Synthesize Insights** (Connect to strategic decisions)
6. **Create Action Plan** (What will you do based on research?)

### Research Methods

**Secondary Research** (existing data):
- Industry reports and analyst research
- Government data and statistics
- Trade association publications
- News and media coverage
- Company websites and materials
- Academic research
- Online databases

**Primary Research** (new data):
- Customer interviews
- Surveys and questionnaires
- Focus groups
- Field observations
- Usability testing
- A/B testing
- Sales conversations

### Data Sources

**Free Sources**:
- U.S. Census Bureau
- Bureau of Labor Statistics
- Industry trade associations
- Google Trends
- Company filings (10-K, S-1)
- News aggregators
- Academic databases (Google Scholar)

**Paid Sources**:
- Gartner, Forrester (analyst firms)
- IBISWorld, Statista (market research)
- Nielsen, comScore (consumer data)
- PitchBook, Crunchbase (startup/funding data)
- SEMrush, SimilarWeb (competitive intelligence)

**AI-Powered Research**:
- Perplexity (cited research aggregation)
- ChatGPT/Claude (synthesis and analysis)
- Consensus (academic research)
- AlphaSense (business intelligence)

## Research Frameworks

### Market Sizing Framework

```
TAM (Total Addressable Market)
├── Approach 1: Top-Down
│   └── Total industry size × our segment %
├── Approach 2: Bottom-Up
│   └── # of potential customers × average revenue
└── Approach 3: Value Theory
    └── Problem value × addressable population

SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market)
└── TAM × reachable with our current capabilities

SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market)
└── SAM × realistic market share we can capture
```

### Market Attractiveness Framework

Evaluate markets on:
1. **Size** - Large enough to matter?
2. **Growth** - Expanding or contracting?
3. **Competition** - How intense and who?
4. **Profitability** - Healthy margins possible?
5. **Access** - Can we reach customers?
6. **Fit** - Match our capabilities?
7. **Timing** - Right time to enter?

Score each factor, weight by importance, rank markets.

## Best Practices

1. **Use Multiple Sources**: Don't rely on single data point
2. **Check Dates**: Ensure data is current (markets change fast)
3. **Question Assumptions**: Especially in market size estimates
4. **Triangulate**: Compare data from different sources
5. **Primary > Secondary**: Talk to real customers when possible
6. **Cite Sources**: Track where data comes from
7. **Update Regularly**: Market research gets stale quickly
8. **Focus**: Don't research everything - target key decisions
9. **Act**: Research should inform action, not delay it
10. **Share**: Democratize insights across organization
