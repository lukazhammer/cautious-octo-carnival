# Competitive Analysis Prompts

## Competitive Intelligence (25 prompts)

### Competitor Identification & Mapping

1. **Complete Competitive Landscape**
   ```
   Map the complete competitive landscape for [product/service] in [market]:

   Identify and categorize:
   - Direct competitors (same solution, same audience)
   - Indirect competitors (different solution, same problem)
   - Substitute products (alternative problem-solving approaches)
   - Emerging competitors (startups, new entrants)
   - Potential competitors (could enter our space)

   For each category:
   - List top 5-10 players
   - Note their primary differentiator
   - Assess threat level (High/Medium/Low)
   ```

2. **Competitor Positioning Analysis**
   ```
   Analyze [competitor]'s positioning and identify weaknesses:

   Research:
   - How do they describe themselves?
   - What value do they claim to deliver?
   - Who do they say they serve?
   - What's their unique angle or differentiator?
   - What does their messaging emphasize?

   Then identify:
   - Gaps in their positioning
   - Overclaimed benefits they don't deliver
   - Audience segments they miss
   - Vulnerabilities in their approach
   - Opportunities for us to differentiate
   ```

3. **Competitive Positioning Comparison**
   ```
   Compare my positioning against competitors:

   My positioning: [your positioning statement]
   Competitor A: [their positioning]
   Competitor B: [their positioning]
   Competitor C: [their positioning]

   Analyze:
   - How is mine different/similar?
   - Is mine distinct and defensible?
   - Where do we overlap (commoditized)?
   - Where am I unique (differentiated)?
   - Am I positioned in white space or red ocean?

   Recommend adjustments for differentiation.
   ```

4. **Competitive Set Definition**
   ```
   Who should I consider my competitive set?

   My product: [description]
   My target audience: [description]
   My primary benefit: [what you deliver]

   Help me identify:
   - Core competitors (fight for same customers)
   - Peripheral competitors (occasional overlap)
   - Aspirational competitors (where we want to be)
   - Dismissed competitors (we ignore but shouldn't)

   Prioritize which competitors to track closely.
   ```

5. **Market Positioning Matrix**
   ```
   Create a positioning matrix for [market/category]:

   Axes to consider:
   - Price (low to high)
   - Feature complexity (simple to advanced)
   - Target size (SMB to Enterprise)
   - Approach (traditional to innovative)
   - Service level (self-service to full-service)

   Choose two axes and plot:
   - Where competitors are positioned
   - Where the gaps/opportunities are
   - Where we are or should be positioned
   - Where the market is moving
   ```

### Competitive Strengths & Weaknesses

6. **SWOT Analysis by Competitor**
   ```
   Conduct a SWOT analysis for [competitor]:

   Strengths:
   - What do they do well?
   - What advantages do they have?
   - What resources/capabilities are strong?

   Weaknesses:
   - What do they do poorly?
   - What gaps exist in their offering?
   - Where are they vulnerable?

   Opportunities (for them):
   - What could they exploit?
   - Where could they expand?

   Threats (to them):
   - What endangers their position?
   - What trends hurt them?

   Implications: How can we exploit their weaknesses and defend against their strengths?
   ```

7. **Feature Comparison Matrix**
   ```
   Create a feature comparison for [product category]:

   Compare across:
   - Our product
   - Competitor A
   - Competitor B
   - Competitor C

   Features to evaluate:
   - [List 15-20 key features]

   For each feature, note:
   - Who has it (✓, ✗, or partial)
   - Quality/depth of implementation
   - Customer importance (High/Med/Low)

   Identify:
   - Table stakes features (everyone has)
   - Differentiating features (few have)
   - Missing features (opportunity gaps)
   ```

8. **Competitive Advantage Analysis**
   ```
   What are [competitor]'s sustainable competitive advantages?

   Analyze:
   - Proprietary technology or IP
   - Network effects or platform advantages
   - Brand strength and recognition
   - Cost advantages or economies of scale
   - Exclusive partnerships or relationships
   - Regulatory advantages or barriers they've cleared
   - Data or insights accumulated over time

   For each advantage:
   - Is it truly defensible?
   - Can we replicate or neutralize it?
   - How durable is it?
   ```

9. **Competitive Disadvantage Identification**
   ```
   Where are we at a competitive disadvantage?

   Honestly assess where competitors beat us:
   - Features or capabilities they have that we lack
   - Advantages we can't easily replicate (brand, scale, network)
   - Resources they have that we don't (capital, team, partnerships)
   - Market position or momentum they've established

   For each disadvantage:
   - How significant is it to customers?
   - Can we close the gap? How and when?
   - Can we reframe it as irrelevant?
   - Can we compete on different dimensions?
   ```

10. **Win/Loss Analysis**
    ```
    Analyze why we win vs. lose against [competitor]:

    When we WIN, it's usually because:
    - [Reason 1]
    - [Reason 2]
    - [Reason 3]

    When we LOSE, it's usually because:
    - [Reason 1]
    - [Reason 2]
    - [Reason 3]

    Patterns to identify:
    - Deal characteristics when we win/lose
    - Customer types where we excel/struggle
    - Scenarios where we have advantage/disadvantage
    - Objections we overcome/can't overcome

    Strategic implications for positioning and sales?
    ```

### Competitive Intelligence Gathering

11. **Competitor Customer Research**
    ```
    How is [competitor] perceived by [target audience]?

    Research:
    - Customer reviews and ratings (what do users say?)
    - Common complaints and praise patterns
    - NPS or satisfaction scores if available
    - Win/loss interview insights
    - Social media sentiment
    - Comparison site rankings and comments

    Synthesize:
    - What are they known for (good and bad)?
    - What do customers wish they did better?
    - Why do customers choose or leave them?
    - Gaps we could exploit
    ```

12. **Competitor Pricing Intelligence**
    ```
    Analyze [competitor]'s pricing strategy:

    Research:
    - Public pricing (if available)
    - Pricing model (one-time, subscription, usage, freemium)
    - Pricing tiers and feature gates
    - Average deal size (if data available)
    - Discounting practices
    - Price changes over time
    - Bundle and package strategies

    Compare to:
    - Our pricing
    - Market averages
    - Value delivered

    Insights on their pricing power and strategy?
    ```

13. **Competitor Content & Messaging Analysis**
    ```
    Analyze [competitor]'s marketing and messaging:

    Review:
    - Website copy and positioning
    - Value propositions emphasized
    - Customer testimonials and case studies
    - Blog content themes and topics
    - Social media presence and voice
    - Ad copy and campaigns
    - Email marketing (if you can access)
    - Sales collateral

    Extract:
    - Core messages and themes
    - Target audience signals
    - Positioning angles
    - Language and terminology used
    - Content gaps or weaknesses
    - Effective approaches to emulate
    ```

14. **Competitor Product Roadmap Intelligence**
    ```
    What's on [competitor]'s product roadmap?

    Gather signals from:
    - Product announcements and press releases
    - Feature request forums or feedback boards
    - Job postings (what skills are they hiring?)
    - Conference presentations and demos
    - Partnership announcements
    - Developer documentation changes
    - Beta or early access programs

    Predict:
    - Features likely coming soon
    - Strategic direction
    - Market segments they're targeting
    - Technology bets they're making

    Should we respond or diverge?
    ```

15. **Competitor Growth & Traction Analysis**
    ```
    How fast is [competitor] growing?

    Estimate traction from:
    - Customer count (if disclosed or estimable)
    - Employee headcount growth (LinkedIn)
    - Funding rounds and valuation
    - Revenue (if public or leaked)
    - Web traffic trends (SimilarWeb, Ahrefs)
    - App download trends
    - Social media follower growth
    - News mentions and media presence

    Growth indicators:
    - Momentum and velocity
    - Market validation
    - Resource availability
    - Competitive threat level
    ```

### Strategic Competitive Response

16. **Competitive White Space Identification**
    ```
    What white space exists in [industry] positioning?

    Map what competitors emphasize:
    - Competitor A focuses on: [positioning angle]
    - Competitor B focuses on: [positioning angle]
    - Competitor C focuses on: [positioning angle]

    Identify unclaimed or underemphasized:
    - Benefits or value propositions
    - Audience segments
    - Use cases or applications
    - Approaches or methodologies
    - Brand personalities or tones

    Where's the white space we could own?
    ```

17. **Differentiation Strategy Development**
    ```
    How should we differentiate from [list of competitors]?

    Given:
    - What they do well
    - Where they're positioned
    - What they emphasize

    We could differentiate on:
    - Different features or capabilities
    - Different target audience
    - Different pricing or business model
    - Different brand personality
    - Different service level
    - Different methodology or approach

    Evaluate each differentiation angle:
    - Defensibility (can competitors copy?)
    - Value to customers (do they care?)
    - Our capability to deliver (can we execute?)
    - Strategic importance (does it matter long-term?)

    Recommend primary and secondary differentiation strategies.
    ```

18. **Competitive Response Strategy**
    ```
    How should we respond to [competitor's action/launch]?

    Their move: [describe competitive action]
    Potential impact on us: [assessment]

    Response options:
    1. Match: Build equivalent capability
    2. Leapfrog: Build better alternative
    3. Ignore: Focus elsewhere, minimize importance
    4. Reframe: Change the conversation
    5. Exploit: Use their move against them

    For each option:
    - Pros and cons
    - Resource requirements
    - Timeline
    - Likelihood of success

    Recommended response and rationale?
    ```

19. **Competitive Positioning Framework**
    ```
    Create a competitive positioning framework for our category:

    Define positioning dimensions:
    - What do customers evaluate when choosing?
    - What drives their decision?
    - What creates differentiation?

    Plot competitors:
    - Where does each player position themselves?
    - What do they claim to be best at?
    - What do customers actually perceive?

    Identify our position:
    - Where should we play?
    - What should we own?
    - How do we frame the choice?

    Create 2-3 positioning maps (different axes).
    ```

20. **Challenger Brand Strategy**
    ```
    We're the challenger brand against [market leader]. What's our strategy?

    Analyze the leader:
    - Their dominance and advantages
    - Their vulnerabilities and constraints
    - Where they're slow or complacent
    - Segments they underserve

    Challenger strategies:
    - Define the leader's weakness as the category problem
    - Serve segments they ignore
    - Out-innovate in specific areas
    - Build community vs. their corporate approach
    - Compete on different value dimensions

    What's our best challenger strategy and why?
    ```

### Advanced Competitive Analysis

21. **Competitive Moats Analysis**
    ```
    What moats protect [competitor] from competition?

    Assess strength of:
    - Network effects (does value increase with users?)
    - Switching costs (hard for customers to leave?)
    - Economies of scale (cost advantages from size?)
    - Brand (strong recognition and preference?)
    - Proprietary technology (hard to replicate?)
    - Regulatory barriers (licenses, compliance?)
    - Data/learning effects (smarter over time?)

    For us:
    - Which moats can we build?
    - Which moats can we bypass or make irrelevant?
    - How long until we have comparable moats?
    ```

22. **Competitive Scenario Planning**
    ```
    How might the competitive landscape evolve?

    Scenario 1 - Consolidation:
    - Which competitors might merge or acquire?
    - How would that change dynamics?
    - Impact on us?

    Scenario 2 - New Entrant:
    - Who could enter (big tech, startups, adjacents)?
    - What would they bring?
    - How would it reshape competition?

    Scenario 3 - Category Disruption:
    - What could make current solutions obsolete?
    - Who would drive that disruption?
    - Where should we place bets?

    Strategic implications for positioning and investment?
    ```

23. **Competitive Business Model Analysis**
    ```
    Compare business models across competitors:

    Analyze for us and key competitors:
    - Revenue model (how money is made)
    - Cost structure (main cost drivers)
    - Customer acquisition approach
    - Scalability (economics as you grow)
    - Margins and profitability
    - Capital requirements
    - Growth levers

    Identify:
    - Most sustainable model
    - Most profitable model
    - Most scalable model
    - Innovative models emerging
    - Our model advantages/disadvantages
    ```

24. **Competitor Pattern Recognition**
    ```
    What patterns exist in how [competitor] operates?

    Observe patterns in:
    - Product release cycles
    - Pricing changes
    - Marketing campaign timing
    - Acquisition strategy
    - Partnership approach
    - Market entry sequence

    Use patterns to:
    - Predict future moves
    - Identify strategic priorities
    - Understand decision-making
    - Find opportunities to counter-position
    - Time our moves advantageously
    ```

25. **Competitive Vulnerability Assessment**
    ```
    Where is [competitor] most vulnerable to disruption?

    Assess vulnerability in:
    - Technology (old stack, technical debt)
    - Business model (changing economics)
    - Customer base (demographic shifts)
    - Distribution (channel disruption)
    - Regulation (new compliance burdens)
    - Talent (key people dependencies)
    - Financial (burn rate, unit economics)

    Exploitation strategies:
    - How could we attack this vulnerability?
    - What would it take?
    - What's the risk/reward?
    - Is this our best opportunity?
    ```

## Competitive Intelligence Sources

### Digital Research

**Company Websites**:
- Positioning, messaging, value props
- Product details and roadmap hints
- Pricing information
- Case studies and customers
- Team and job postings

**Review Sites**:
- G2, Capterra, TrustRadius (B2B)
- Amazon, Yelp, Google (B2C)
- Customer sentiment and complaints
- Feature comparisons
- Use case insights

**Social Media**:
- LinkedIn (company updates, employees)
- Twitter (announcements, customer service)
- Facebook, Instagram (consumer brands)
- Reddit (honest customer opinions)

**Traffic & Analytics**:
- SimilarWeb (traffic, engagement, sources)
- Ahrefs, SEMrush (SEO, keywords, backlinks)
- BuiltWith (technology stack)

### Business Intelligence

**Funding & Financials**:
- Crunchbase, PitchBook (funding, valuation)
- Public filings (10-K, S-1 for public companies)
- Press releases (announcements)

**News & Media**:
- Google News, Google Alerts
- Industry publications
- Company blogs
- Podcast appearances

**Job Postings**:
- LinkedIn Jobs
- Company careers pages
- Indeed, Glassdoor
- Signals about priorities and roadmap

### Primary Research

**Customer Conversations**:
- Win/loss interviews
- Former competitor customers
- Current competitor customers (ethically)

**Sales Intelligence**:
- Competitive deals
- Sales team debriefs
- Demo recordings

**Market Events**:
- Conferences and trade shows
- Webinars and demos
- Analyst briefings

## Best Practices

1. **Stay Ethical**: Don't lie, steal, or deceive to gather intelligence
2. **Focus**: Track 3-5 key competitors deeply vs. 20 superficially
3. **Update Regularly**: Intelligence gets stale fast
4. **Distribute**: Share insights with sales, product, marketing teams
5. **Act**: Intelligence should drive strategy and tactics
6. **Customer Lens**: Focus on customer perception, not just company claims
7. **Patterns Over Points**: Look for trends, not just snapshots
8. **Validate**: Triangulate from multiple sources
9. **Organize**: Keep a central repository of competitive intel
10. **Learn**: Study what works for others, adapt for yourself
